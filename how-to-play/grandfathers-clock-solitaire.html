How to Play Grandfather's Clock Solitaire

Grandfather's Clock Solitaire Rules

| Players: 1 | Type: Solitaire | Supplies: 1 Deck |

Objective

The objective of Grandfather's Clock is to build the foundation piles up in ascending order until the top card of each pile corresponds with the numbers of an analog clock in the correct position.

Setup

Remove the nine of clubs, ten of hearts, jack of spades, queen of diamonds, king of clubs, two of hearts, three of spades, four of diamonds, five of clubs, six of hearts, seven of spades, and eight of diamonds from the deck.

Using these twelve cards, form the twelve foundation piles in a circle, resembling a clock dial. Place the nine of clubs at the 12 o'clock position, the ten of hearts will be at the 1 o'clock position, and continue until the eight of diamonds is placed at the 11 o'clock position.

Deal the remaining deck into eight tableau piles, each with five face-up cards, splayed downward.

Game Play

Foundation piles are built up in ascending order and in suit. An ace may be played on a king to continue the sequence. To add a card to a foundation pile, it must be one rank higher and of the same suit as the top card of the pile it is being moved to. The foundation piles at the 1, 2, 3, and 4 o'clock positions are built up to five cards; all other foundation piles are built up to four cards.

The top card of each tableau pile is available for play either onto a foundation pile or onto another tableau pile. Only one card may be moved at a time. Tableau piles may be built down in sequence, regardless of suit. You may move the top card of one tableau pile onto another tableau pile as long as the card being moved is one rank lower and of the same suit as the top card of the pile it is being moved to. Kings may be played on aces to continue the sequence.

When a tableau pile becomes empty, it may be filled with any available card. When there are no more available moves, the game is over.

To Win

Grandfather's Clock is won if all cards are built onto the foundation piles, resembling the same numbers on a dial clock; Ace represents 1, 2-10 represent their face value, Jack representing 11, and the queen representing 12. If there are no more available moves and the foundation piles are not complete, the game is lost.
