How to Play Cat's Cradle Solitaire

Cats cradle solitaire is a difficult solitaire game where you have to deplete the stock to win.

Cat's Cradle Solitaire Rules

| Players: 1 | Type: Solitaire | Supplies: 1 Deck |

Objective

The objective of Cat's Cradle is to empty the stockpile by building tableau piles down in descending order and alternating color.

Setup

Deal out 16 face-up cards in a 4x4 grid, to form the tableau of 16 piles. Place the remaining deck face-down to form the stock.

Game Play

Only the top card of each tableau pile is available for play. Tableau piles may be built down in descending order and alternating color. To add a card to another tableau pile, it must be one rank lower and of the opposite color than the top card of the tableau pile it is being moved onto.

You may only move a card from one tableau pile to another if both piles are on the same vertical, horizontal, or diagonal line. For example, the 7 of diamonds could be placed onto the 8 of spades because they are on the same horizontal line, or the 8 of clubs because they are on the same vertical line. The four of clubs could be played on the 5 of hearts because they are on the same diagonal line.

When there are no more available moves you want to make, deal out a card from the stock, face-up, to each empty tableau space.

To Win

Cat's Cradle is won if all cards are dealt from the stock to the tableau. If there are no more available moves and there are still cards in the stock without the ability to move more cards to the tableau, the game is lost.
