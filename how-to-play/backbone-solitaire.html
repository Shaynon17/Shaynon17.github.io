How to Play Backbone Solitaire

Backbone Solitaire Rules

| Players: 1 | Type: Solitaire | Supplies: 2 Decks |

Objective

The objective of Backbone solitaire is to build the eight foundation piles from the ace up to the king in each suit. There will be eight foundation piles total, with two per suit.

Setup

Deal two columns side by side in the center to form the reserve piles, known as the 'backbone.'' Each column will have 11 face-up cards, splayed downward. Place one card at the bottom of these two columns that overlaps both bottom cards of the columns; this card is referred to as the 'coccyx.'

On each side of the backbone, place four cards face up to form the tableau piles, known as the 'ribs,' for a total of eight tableau piles. Place the remaining deck face down to form the stock.

Game Play

There will be eight foundation piles that build up from ace to king in each suit, with two foundation piles per suit. When an ace becomes available, place it above the tableau to form a foundation pile.

Cards in the 'backbone' can be moved to a foundation pile or a tableau pile (“rib”). Only the top card of each backbone reserve pile is available for play. The single card (“coccyx”) at the base of the 'backbone' must be moved to a foundation pile or a tableau pile before the top cards of the two columns become available for play.

The tableau ('rib') piles are built down in sequence and by suit. When a tableau pile is empty, any available card from the backbone or waste pile may fill this spot.

When there are no more available moves the player wants to make, flip the top card of the stock over to form or add to the waste pile. The top card of the waste pile is available for play onto a foundation pile or onto a tableau pile.

When the stock is depleted, you may redeal by flipping over the waste pile to form a new stock. You may redeal twice per game.

To Win

Backbone is won when all cards are built onto the foundation piles. If you run out of available moves before all cards are on the foundation piles, you lose.
