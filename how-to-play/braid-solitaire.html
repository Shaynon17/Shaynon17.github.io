How to Play Braid Solitaire

Braid Solitaire Rules

| Players: 1 | Type: Solitaire | Supplies: 2 Decks |

Objective

The objective of braid solitaire is to diminish the reserve and build the eight foundation piles from ace to king in suit.

Setup

Twenty cards are dealt face up and overlapping each other in a braid to form the reserve. Twelve tableau piles are each dealt one card. Two of the tableau piles are dealt on each side of the braid, forming the four inner tableau piles. Deal two columns of four cards each on both sides of the braid to form the eight outer tableau piles. Deal the next card above the tableau to form the first foundation pile. Place the remaining cards face down to form the stock.

Game Play

The first card dealt to start a foundation pile determines the starting rank for all other foundation piles. When another card of the same rank as this first card becomes available, it is moved above the tableau to start another foundation pile. Foundation piles are built up in sequence and in suit from the starting card until each contains 13 cards. There are two foundation piles per suit. Aces may be played on kings to continue the sequence.

Cards may only be moved to the foundation pile. All cards in each tableau pile, the top card of the waste, and the top card of the reserve are available for play only onto a foundation pile. Tableau piles may never contain more than one card.

When an outside tableau pile becomes empty, fill it with the top card of the waste, or with the top card of the stock if the waste is empty. When an inner tableau pile becomes empty, fill it with the top card of the reserve.

When there are no more moves available that you want to make, flip the top card of the stock to form or add to the waste pile. When the stock is depleted, you may redeal by flipping over the waste pile to form a new stock. You may redeal only twice.

To Win

To win Braid, all cards must be built onto the eight foundation piles.
