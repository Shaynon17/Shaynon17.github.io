How to Play Gaps Solitaire

Gaps Solitaire Rules

| Players: 1 | Type: Solitaire | Supplies: 1 Deck |

Objective

The objective of Gaps is to rearrange the rows of cards until each row starts from 2 on the left and continues up in ascending order to the king, and in suit, at the end of the row on the right.

Setup

Deal all cards into four rows of 13 cards each, all face-up, then remove all aces from the layout, leaving four gaps. Place the aces aside until the cards are redealt.

Game Play

Any card may be moved into a gap as long as the card is one rank higher and of the same suit as the card to the left of the gap, or one rank lower and of the same suit as the card to the right of the gap. A 2 may be moved to a gap that is at the start of a row.

When there are no more available moves the player wants to make, they may redeal. All cards that are not in the proper sequence to win the game are redealt. Cards are in the proper sequence when a 2 of any suit starts a row, and the subsequent cards to its right are one rank higher and of the same suit. For example, if the top row starts with a 2 of hearts, and then on its right has a 3 of hearts, then a 4 of hearts, then a 5 of hearts, then a card that is not in the proper ascending order and suit like an 8 of clubs, and so on, all cards after the 5 of hearts in this row would be part of the cards redealt. Another example would be if a row doesn't start with a 2, then that entire row would be part of the redeal.

To redeal, gather all cards that are not in the proper sequence of each row, and the four aces. Shuffle these cards together and deal them out to the tableau to fill in the thirteen-by-four grid, and remove the aces again to create four new gaps. Continue play as normal. You may redeal twice per game.

To Win

Gaps is won if all four rows start with a 2 on the left, and each card to its right is one rank higher and of the same suit as the preceding card, until the last card in the row is a king. If there are no more available moves after the second redeal, and the rows are not all in the proper sequence, the game is lost.

Variation

There is a common variation to Gaps that does not allow you to play a card into a gap based on the card to the right of the gap, only the left. Meaning you may only move a card into a gap as long as the card is one rank higher and of the same suit as the card to the left of the gap. All other rules are the same.
