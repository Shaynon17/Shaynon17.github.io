How to Play Casket Solitaire

Casket Solitaire Rules

| Players: 1 | Type: Solitaire | Supplies: 2 Decks |

Objective

The objective of Casket is to build the eight foundation piles up in ascending order to the king in suit.

Setup

Deal 13 cards face down to a reserve pile in the center, and flip the top card face-up. This reserve pile is known as the “jewels.” Next, deal 13 piles with one card each in the shape of a casket. Five of the piles will be dealt above the jewels reserve pile, to form what is called the “lid” of the casket; these are the “lid” reserve piles. Deal two tableau piles on each side of the jewels reserve pile to form the walls, and four tableau piles at the base to complete the casket shape. The walls and the base of the casket form the eight tableau piles. Place the remaining deck face-up to form the stock.

Game Play

When an ace becomes available for play, move it outside the tableau to form a foundation pile. There are eight foundation piles, two per suit, that are built from the ace up in ascending order to the king.

Only one card may be moved at a time from any pile to another pile. The top card of each of the eight tableau piles, the top card of each of the three waste piles, the top card of the stock, and all five cards in the “lid” piles are available for play onto a foundation pile or one of the eight tableau piles.

The five “lid” reserve piles may only hold one card per pile at all times. When one of the five lid piles becomes empty, it is immediately filled from the top card of the “jewel” reserve pile. If the top card of the “jewel” pile is face down, turn it face up. When the “jewel” pile is empty, empty spaces in the “lid” row may be filled by the top card from any tableau pile, or the top card of any waste pile.

Tableau piles may be built down in descending order and in suit. To add a card to a tableau pile, the card must be one rank lower and of the same suit as the top card of the tableau pile it is being moved onto.

For example, the 5 of hearts in the lid reserve pile at the top of the casket may be moved onto the 6 of hearts tableau pile at the bottom of the casket. Since the 5 of hearts creates an empty space in the lid, the top card of the jewels reserve pile, which is the 9 of spades, is moved to fill this empty space. Following this example, the 4 of hearts may now be played onto the 5 of hearts. Since the 4 of hearts creates an empty space in the tableau, and there are no current waste piles to choose a top card from, the top card of the stock is used to fill this empty tableau space.

When there are no more available moves the player wants to make, flip the top card of the stock onto any of the three waste piles. The top card of each waste pile is available for play onto a tableau pile or foundation pile. When the stock is depleted and there are no more moves, the game is over.

To Win

To win Casket, all cards must be built onto the eight foundation piles. If the stock is depleted, and there are no more moves and the foundation piles are not completed, the game is lost.

Variation

-A harder variation would be that once the jewels pile is empty, the lid piles may not be filled by any card.
